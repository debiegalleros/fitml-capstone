The problem

35% of online clothing orders get returned, versus 8% in physical stores.

That gap costs retailers over $100 billion annually. Sizing uncertainty drives most of this, though shoppers also hesitate simply because they can't see how a garment will look on them before buying.

How sizing works

Enter your measurements once — height, weight (optional), bust, waist, hip, body type. FitML matches you to a size using the garment's size chart, then verifies that match with a model trained on real fit feedback from past purchases.

  • Several modeling approaches were tested; the best-performing one is in use.
  • When a recommendation sits near a size boundary, FitML flags it — lower confidence, brief explanation of the tradeoff — rather than guessing.

How the try-on works

Upload a photo. Full-body works best; half-body is fine for tops and dresses. FitML reads your pose, then an AI image model renders the actual catalog garment on your photo — like a mirror shot of you wearing it.

  • Want your face left out entirely? Check "Crop my face out of stored photos" on the profile page and everything above your nose is cropped out before your photo is even saved, so no face pixels ever reach storage, pose detection, or the try-on preview.
  • The preview shows how the garment looks worn on you; how your chosen size will fit comes from the size recommendation and fit advice.

Why fairness matters here

Most sizing tools are a black box — a recommendation with no way to check it's reliable across body types. FitML's model was tested directly against that standard, and audited for the reasoning behind its predictions. This surfaced a real performance gap for certain groups, which was corrected and verified with before-and-after results.

Privacy

Your photo is used only to pose the try-on preview — it never feeds the sizing model. An opt-in checkbox on the profile page lets you crop everything above your nose out of your photo before it's even saved, so no face pixels ever reach storage, pose detection, or the try-on preview. If you leave that box unchecked, we still take steps to keep your actual face accurate in every try-on preview rather than leaving it to chance. Uploads are stored for at most 24 hours and then automatically deleted, and one session's photos can't be retrieved by another. This follows the proportionality, transparency, and retention-limit principles of the Philippines' Data Privacy Act of 2012 (RA 10173).
